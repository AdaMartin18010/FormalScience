Context-free grammars are core tools in theoretical computer science, compiler theory, and natural language processing. However, their expressive power is limitedâ€”they cannot describe cross-level dependencies or context-sensitive phenomena. Chomsky and Greibach normal forms provide standardized foundations for algorithmic implementation and theoretical analysis. LL/LR parsing methods have enabled efficient parser engineering, but impose strict constraints on grammar structure. Recently, extended CFGs (e.g., attribute grammars, probabilistic CFGs) have been proposed to meet more complex application needs.
